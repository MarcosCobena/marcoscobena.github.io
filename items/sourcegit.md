Watching these fantastic Blender's video logs ([BLENDERHEADS](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLa1F2ddGya_8I8QCMCKlQUOQpgOga2nZ-) and [Blender Studio Logs](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLav47HAVZMjkxzzLDqYpTCosbsWjosUN0)) I heard they were switching from VS Code's Git to an external app more UI-friendly.

It has been a year or so asking my-self which app would it be, because I would like to give it a try. Fork (paid license :-) ) has been my prefered choice since 2020? but it is not Open Source, and is not available on Linux.

![](items/images/sourcegit.png)

This week [I asked Blender](https://mastodon.social/@MarcosCobena/116283504679248737) about their app and Sybren (thank you again!) found it for me: [SourceGit](https://sourcegit-scm.github.io). I have been using it all day since then and, for now, it feels a good replacement:
- faster: to boot up, for example
- lighter: to move between repositories, fetch from origin, etc. I also have the impression RAM consumption is a little bit lower
- available on Linux :-)

For my own joy, it is made in C# (.NET) with Avalonia, so I am one step closer to such a great community Blender is.
